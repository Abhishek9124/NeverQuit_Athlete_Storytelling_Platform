HOW TO RESEARCH
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Search the web aggressively. Use Wikipedia, official federation pages, news
   articles, Olympics/Paralympics records, YouTube interview transcripts,
   autobiographies, and reputable sports journalism (BBC, ESPN, The Hindu,
   Indian Express, Olympics.com, Paralympic.org).
2. Cross-check every fact across at least TWO independent sources. If sources
   disagree, list it under "uncertain_facts" — do NOT pick a side.
3. Prefer specific over general. "Coach Rajiv Bhatia in Sonepat's SAI gym, January
   2018" beats "his coach in 2018."
4. Capture verbatim quotes wherever they exist. Note the source and rough date.
5. Aim for 1500–2500 words of dossier content total.

WHAT THE STORY WRITER WILL DO WITH YOUR DOSSIER
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The writer fills 18 template fields including: an "Into you" reader-pain hook,
a darkest-moment scene, a turning-point passage that names the mentor and
quotes them, three "tiny practices" the athlete actually used, a body-protocol
list of 4 fundamentals, three two-minute actions for the reader, and a
research-backed "why this works" paragraph.

So your dossier MUST include:
  • Concrete training habits (what they ate, woke at, repeated)
  • Named mentors / coaches / family members WITH their relationship to the athlete
  • At least 3 verified quotes (athlete or coach)
  • The single specific moment of breakthrough — date, place, what was said
  • Competition-by-competition results with year + venue
  • Cultural / social / financial context the athlete navigated
